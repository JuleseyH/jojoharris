Layout notes for Jo
Jo's Notes first. The emotional hook — why Jo designed it, when she wears it — comes before anything functional. This is what makes people connect with the piece and want to buy it.
Trust signals directly below the buy button. Delivery, hallmarking, returns and payment logos are all visible without scrolling past the purchase decision. Currently these are scattered or missing.
Hallmark thumbnail in the image gallery. The hallmark is mentioned in the description but never shown. A close-up shot of it is a powerful quality signal that competitors rarely use.
Star rating near the buy button. Reviews currently sit at the very bottom of the page below Related Products. Moving them up to near the price significantly increases their impact on conversion.
Current layout issue: functional specs first. Right now the page leads with materials, measurements and made-to-order notes. These are important but they shouldn't be the first thing a customer reads.
Current layout issue: no accordions. Shipping, returns and care notes currently sit as plain text blocks. On mobile this creates a very long page that is hard to scan. Accordions keep it clean and professional.
Full stop in product titles. Small thing but it looks unfinished in a product listing. Remove from all product titles across the site.
Collection label. Linking each product back to its collection (Soli Salva, Pearly Queen etc.) adds context and encourages browsing — currently this link isn't visible on product pages.
