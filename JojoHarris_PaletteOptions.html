Choosing your action colour

Jo, this one is your call. At the moment several golds, black and a stray blue all compete on the page, so nothing leads the eye toward buying. The idea here is to give your whole purchase path one colour, used nowhere else, so a shopper is led by colour from the first "Shop the collection" button, through Add to Cart, to the cart, and all the way to Place Order. Every step on the way to buying wears the same colour, and nothing else does. That's what trains the eye: this colour means "a step closer to owning it". Below is one of your product cards shown with four different action colours. Your gold, cream and olive stay exactly as they are. Have a look on your phone and on a larger screen if you can, and see which feels most like you. There's no wrong answer; they all work and all pass accessibility. Two strengths of the action colour

One more thing worth understanding. To keep the action colour powerful, it works in two strengths. The solid version is for the single most important next step on a page, only one per screen. The outline version is for other "keep shopping" steps like collection buttons, so they clearly belong to the journey without competing with the main action. Anything that isn't part of shopping at all stays neutral. (Shown in teal, it adapts to whichever colour you choose.)

On a product page
Solid: the one most important action on this page.
Outline: a "keep shopping" step. Same colour, lighter weight.
Neutral: not a step toward buying, so it stays out of the action colour.
On the homepage
Solid: the single primary call to action in the hero.
Outline: individual collection buttons. Part of the journey, but not competing with the main action.
Neutral: content, not shopping. Stays neutral.
